Overview

Purpose

How can a cowboy heritage craft be shared with the whole world? My father-in-law is a renowned bridle bit and spur maker. He has taught me some of his craft, and I am passionate about learning it and sharing it with my children. It is part of their legacy. I will create a website where my father-in-law can display his art and the items he has for sale. This will allow his unique style and amazing talent to be showcased and enjoyedâ€”including his posterity.

Audience

My audience for this website is potential customers, western heritage art collectors, and family and friends of Mark Dahl. His gear is fully operational and sought after by buckaroos everywhere. It is also beautiful art, and serious art collectors of western gear would also want his pieces to add to a collection. Finally, those close to Mark, value his work and want to see his latest pieces. Since many of these people are far away, this website gives them a chance to stay current with his work.

Images for the Home page

Buckaroo Spurs #559
These buckaroo style spurs are made of polished, blued steel. The bands are 5/8 of an inch wide, inlayed with engraved sterling silver on the outside and engraved in the steel on the inside. They have 2 1/4 inch shanks which are silvered and engraved on both sides and the top. The rowels are 2 1/4 inches with 16 points. With the long bands and high, swinging buttons, they will hang very well on a boot and they do have posts to attach heel chains that would probably never be needed.

Ring Bit #533
Made of polished blued steel. Inlayed and overlayed with sterling silver. It has a 5 1/8 inch loose-jawed spoon spade mouthpiece. From the center of the bar to the top of the spoon is 4 3/4 inches. From the center of the bar to where the headstall hooks on is 2 1/4 inches. From the center of the bar to where the reins hook on is 4 3/4 inches. Weighs 1 pound 9 1/2 ounces.
